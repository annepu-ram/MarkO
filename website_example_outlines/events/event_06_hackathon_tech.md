# Hackathon / Tech Conference / Startup Event

> Focus: Innovation-driven events bringing together developers, entrepreneurs, and tech enthusiasts to build, learn, and pitch

## Real Website Examples & Key Insights

| Site | URL | Key Pattern |
|------|-----|-------------|
| HackMIT | hackmit.org | Bold countdown hero, sponsor tier grid, clean registration flow |
| TechCrunch Disrupt | techcrunch.com/events/disrupt | Speaker lineup tabs, startup battlefield section, multi-track schedule |
| PyCon | pycon.org | Talk schedule accordion, sponsor ticker, community-first messaging |
| Y Combinator Demo Day | ycombinator.com/demoday | Startup batch grid, investor-focused CTAs, clean minimal design |
| MLH (Major League Hacking) | mlh.io | Hackathon season stats, FAQ accordion, beginner-friendly onboarding |

**Patterns to incorporate:**
- Countdown to event start is a must — creates urgency for registration deadlines
- Speaker and judge lineups displayed in grids with headshots build credibility
- Multi-track schedules work best in tabs (by track) or accordion (by time block)
- Counter-up stats (participants, projects built, prizes awarded) showcase impact
- Sponsor tiers in columnsgrid reinforce funding and attract future sponsors
- FAQ accordion reduces support burden and answers registration/logistics questions
- Clear prize breakdown motivates participation

## Variant A — Hackathon / Code Competition

**Page: Home (Single Page)**

1. **titlebar**
   Hackathon logo, nav (About, Tracks, Prizes, Judges, Schedule, FAQ, Sponsors), button ("Register Now").

2. **layout-row (Hero)**
   Left: eyebrow ("48 Hours. One Weekend. Endless Possibilities.") + heading ("CodeStorm Hackathon 2026") + paragraph ("Build something extraordinary. 500 hackers, 48 hours, $50,000 in prizes. In-person at the Innovation Hub, Downtown.") + button ("Apply to Hack") + button ("Become a Mentor"). Right: image (team huddled around laptops at 2 AM with energy drinks and sticky notes).

3. **br (minimal divider)**
   Visual separator between hero and content sections.

4. **countdown (Hacking Begins)**
   Countdown timer to the hackathon kickoff with heading: "Hacking Starts In".

5. **counter-up (Hackathon Impact)**
   "500 Hackers", "$50,000 in Prizes", "48 Hours", "120 Projects Built Last Year".

6. **tabs (Challenge Tracks)**
   Tabs: AI & Machine Learning, Climate & Sustainability, Health Tech, Open Innovation. Each tab: heading (track name) + paragraph (problem statement and what judges are looking for) + image (relevant imagery — AI visualizations, green tech, medical devices) + badge ("$15,000 Grand Prize" per track) + caption (track sponsor name).

7. **columnsgrid (Prizes — 3 columns)**
   Each: icon + heading (placement — "Grand Prize", "Runner-Up", "Best Beginner Hack") + counter-up (prize amount) + paragraph (inclusions — cash, cloud credits, hardware, incubator spots) + badge ("New" on special category prizes).

8. **layout-row (Judges & Mentors)**
   Left: heading ("Meet the Judges") + paragraph ("Industry leaders evaluating your creations on innovation, technical execution, and impact"). Right: columnsgrid (3 columns) with image (judge headshot) + heading (name) + caption (title, company — e.g., "CTO, TechCorp") + badge ("Lead Judge" on one).

9. **accordion (48-Hour Schedule)**
   Panels: "Friday 6 PM — Check-in & Opening Ceremony", "Friday 8 PM — Hacking Begins + Team Formation", "Saturday 9 AM — Workshops & Mentor Hours", "Saturday 6 PM — Midpoint Check-in", "Saturday 11 PM — Late Night Fuel & Mini Challenges", "Sunday 10 AM — Code Freeze & Submission", "Sunday 12 PM — Demo Presentations", "Sunday 3 PM — Judging & Awards Ceremony".

10. **carousel (Past Hackathon Moments)**
   Photos: teams presenting demos, late-night coding sessions, winners holding oversized checks, workshop sessions, mentors reviewing code, post-event group photo.

11. **accordion (FAQ)**
    Panels: "Who can participate?", "Do I need a team?", "What should I bring?", "Is food provided?", "Can I start working on my project beforehand?", "What are the judging criteria?", "Is there a code of conduct?".

12. **ticker (Sponsor Logos)**
    Scrolling logos: title sponsor, cloud platform partner, developer tools companies, venture capital firms, food and beverage sponsors.

13. **form (Hacker Application)**
    textbox (full name), textbox (email), textbox (GitHub or portfolio URL), dropdown (experience level: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced), dropdown (preferred track: AI/ML, Climate, Health Tech, Open Innovation), radio (team status: "I have a team", "Looking for teammates", "Solo"), textarea (tell us about a project you are proud of), checkbox ("I agree to the Code of Conduct and event terms"), button ("Submit Application").

14. **layout-row (Footer)**
    Hackathon organizer name and logo, venue address and dates, contact email, social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Discord, GitHub), link ("Code of Conduct"), link ("Press Kit"), link ("Past Winners"), copyright.

## Variant B — Tech Conference / Developer Summit

**Page: Home (Single Page)**

1. **titlebar**
   Conference logo, nav (Speakers, Agenda, Workshops, Tickets, Venue, Sponsors, Contact), button ("Get Tickets").

2. **layout-row (Hero)**
   Full-width image (large conference stage with speaker presenting to a packed auditorium, screens showing code) + overlay: eyebrow ("September 18-20, 2026 | Convention Center") + heading ("DevForward Summit 2026") + paragraph ("Three days of talks, workshops, and networking with 2,000 developers and 80+ speakers shaping the future of software") + button ("View Speakers") + button ("Buy Tickets").

3. **br (minimal divider)**
   Visual separator between hero and content sections.

4. **counter-up (Conference Scale)**
   "80+ Speakers", "2,000 Attendees", "3 Days", "6 Tracks".

5. **countdown (Conference Opens)**
   Countdown timer with heading: "Summit Begins In".

6. **tabs (Speaker Lineup by Track)**
   Tabs: Frontend, Backend & Infrastructure, AI & Data, DevOps & Cloud, Security, Leadership. Each tab: columnsgrid (3 columns) with image (speaker headshot) + heading (speaker name) + caption (talk title) + paragraph (company and role) + badge ("Keynote" on select speakers).

7. **layout-row (Keynote Spotlight)**
   Left: image (keynote speaker portrait, large). Right: eyebrow ("Opening Keynote") + heading (speaker name) + paragraph (bio — notable achievements, current role, what they will talk about) + blockquote ("A short powerful quote from the speaker about the conference theme") + badge ("Author of [Book Title]") + button ("Add to My Schedule").

8. **accordion (Three-Day Agenda)**
   Panels: "Day 1 — Thursday, Sep 18 (Workshops & Welcome)", "Day 2 — Friday, Sep 19 (Main Conference)", "Day 3 — Saturday, Sep 20 (Deep Dives & Closing)". Each panel: paragraph with time slots, session titles, speaker names, room assignments, and track labels.

9. **columnsgrid (Workshop Highlights — 3 columns)**
   Each: image (workshop topic illustration) + heading (workshop title — "Building with Rust", "LLM Fine-Tuning Masterclass", "Kubernetes at Scale") + caption (instructor name, duration — "3 hours") + paragraph (prerequisites, what you will build) + button ("Reserve Spot").

10. **layout-row (Ticket Tiers)**
   columnsgrid (3 columns): heading ("Early Bird", "Standard", "VIP") + paragraph (inclusions — sessions access, workshop slots, networking dinner, speaker meet-and-greet) + counter-up (price) + badge ("Save 30%" on Early Bird) + button ("Purchase").

11. **layout-row (Venue & Travel)**
    Left: heading ("The Venue") + paragraph (convention center details, address, capacity, nearby hotels, airport shuttle info) + icon (map pin) + caption (full address) + link ("View on Google Maps"). Right: image (venue exterior or lobby).

12. **ticker (Past Speaker Companies)**
    Scrolling logos: Google, Microsoft, Stripe, Vercel, Cloudflare, Netflix, Shopify, GitHub — companies whose engineers have spoken at previous editions.

13. **carousel (Past Conference Photos)**
    Photos: packed keynote hall, hallway conversations, workshop rooms, sponsor booths, after-party, swag table.

14. **form (Stay Informed / Group Registration)**
    textbox (full name), textbox (email), textbox (company name), dropdown (role: Developer, Engineering Manager, CTO/VP, Designer, Student, Other), dropdown (interest: "Attending", "Speaking", "Sponsoring", "Group Registration"), checkbox ("Send me speaker announcements and schedule updates"), button ("Submit").

15. **layout-row (Footer)**
    Conference organizer and community name, dates and venue, contact email, social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, YouTube, Mastodon), link ("Speaker CFP"), link ("Sponsorship Prospectus"), link ("Code of Conduct"), link ("Accessibility"), copyright.
