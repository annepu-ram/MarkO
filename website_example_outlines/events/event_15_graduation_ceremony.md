# Graduation / Convocation / Commencement

> Focus: Ceremony logistics, pride-driven emotional storytelling, and guest/graduate information dissemination

## Real Website Examples & Key Insights

| Site | URL | Key Pattern |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Harvard Commencement | commencement.harvard.edu | Schedule timeline, speaker announcement, livestream CTA, guest FAQ |
| Stanford Commencement | commencement.stanford.edu | Ceremony details, photo galleries, Class of 2026 stats, parking/logistics |
| IIT Bombay Convocation | iitb.ac.in/convocation | Chief guest profile, degree-wise schedule, registration portal, live webcast |
| University of Oxford | ox.ac.uk/students/graduation | Ceremony booking, dress code guide, guest tickets, photography service |

**Patterns to incorporate:**
- Countdown to ceremony date creates anticipation
- Chief guest / keynote speaker spotlight is a major draw
- Detailed logistics (venue, dress code, parking, guest limits) reduce confusion
- Graduate stats (class size, degrees awarded, departments) build pride
- Livestream link for remote family members
- Photo gallery from past ceremonies sets expectations
- FAQ section is essential (guest tickets, regalia, accessibility)

## Variant A — University Graduation Ceremony

**Page: Home (Single Page)**

1. **titlebar**
   University logo + "Convocation 2026", nav (Schedule, Speaker, Graduates, Gallery, Livestream, FAQ), button ("Graduate Registration").

2. **layout-row (Hero)**
   Left: eyebrow ("Class of 2026") + heading ("47th Annual Convocation Ceremony") + paragraph ("A milestone celebration honoring the achievements of 3,200 graduates. Join us as we celebrate years of dedication, learning, and growth.") + button ("View Schedule") + button ("Watch Livestream"). Right: image (graduates in cap and gown throwing hats in the air, or processional shot).

3. **br (divider)**
   Visual separator between hero and content sections.

4. **countdown (Ceremony Countdown)**
   heading ("Convocation Day") + countdown timer + caption ("June 15, 2026 | University Main Auditorium | 10:00 AM").

5. **counter-up (Class of 2026 Stats)**
   "3,200 Graduates", "45 Departments", "12 PhD Scholars", "150+ Gold Medalists", "38 Countries Represented".

6. **layout-row (Chief Guest / Keynote Speaker)**
   Left: image (speaker portrait). Right: eyebrow ("Keynote Speaker") + heading (speaker name) + caption (title -- "Nobel Laureate / CEO / Cabinet Minister") + paragraph (biography -- achievements, connection to the university, message theme) + blockquote ("Education is not the filling of a pail, but the lighting of a fire.") + caption (speaker name).

7. **tabs (Ceremony Schedule)**
   Tabs: Morning Session, Afternoon Session, Evening Reception. Morning: accordion -- "9:00 AM Academic Procession", "9:30 AM Welcome Address by Vice-Chancellor", "10:00 AM Keynote Address", "10:45 AM Conferring of Honorary Degrees", "11:00 AM Degree Conferral -- School of Engineering", "11:30 AM Degree Conferral -- School of Sciences". Afternoon: "2:00 PM School of Arts & Humanities", "2:30 PM School of Business", "3:00 PM School of Medicine", "3:30 PM Gold Medal Ceremony", "4:00 PM Valedictory Address". Evening: "6:00 PM Alumni Reception", "7:00 PM Cultural Program", "8:00 PM Dinner".

8. **columnsgrid (Graduate Information -- 3 columns)**
   Each: icon + heading + paragraph. "Academic Regalia" (gown collection dates, return policy, dress code underneath -- formal attire), "Guest Passes" (each graduate receives 4 guest passes, collection from department office), "Photography" (official photographer stationed at stage, photo packages available for pre-order).

9. **layout-row (Livestream & Virtual Attendance)**
   Left: heading ("Can't Be There in Person?") + paragraph ("Watch the full ceremony live. Share the link with family and friends around the world.") + button ("Watch Livestream") + caption ("Available on YouTube and university website"). Right: image (screenshot of livestream setup or family watching on laptop).

10. **carousel (Past Convocation Gallery)**
   Photos: procession, degree conferral on stage, proud graduates, families celebrating, group photos, campus decorations.

11. **layout-row (Venue & Logistics)**
    heading ("Venue & Practical Information") + columnsgrid (3 columns): each with icon + heading + paragraph. "Venue" (University Main Auditorium, Sector 25 Campus -- gates open at 8:00 AM), "Parking" (designated lots P1 and P2, shuttle service from P3, arrive early), "Accessibility" (wheelchair ramps, reserved seating, sign language interpretation available).

12. **accordion (FAQ)**
    "How many guests can each graduate bring?", "What is the dress code?", "Where do I collect my gown?", "Will the ceremony be livestreamed?", "Can I take photos during the ceremony?", "What if it rains? (outdoor backup plan)", "When will I receive my degree certificate?", "Is there parking available?".

13. **form (Graduate Registration / RSVP)**
    textbox (graduate full name), textbox (roll number / student ID), dropdown (department/school), textbox (email), textbox (phone), textbox (number of guests attending -- max 4), dropdown (attending reception: Yes / No), checkbox (I need accessibility accommodations), textarea (special requirements), button ("Confirm Registration").

14. **layout-row (Footer)**
    columnsgrid: Column 1 -- university logo + paragraph (university motto). Column 2 -- links (Schedule, Speaker, Gallery, FAQ, Livestream). Column 3 -- links (Academic Regalia, Guest Passes, Photography, Contact). Column 4 -- caption (convocation helpdesk phone) + caption (email) + icon (social links).
